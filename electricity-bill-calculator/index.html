🔍 How the K-Electric Bill Calculator Works

Calculation Method

Our calculator uses the official K-Electric tariff structure for 2026:

  1. Slab-Based Calculation: Units are charged progressively across different slabs (0-50, 51-100, 101-200, etc.)
  2. GST Addition: 17% General Sales Tax applied to total energy charges
  3. Meter Rent: Rs. 75-150 for single-phase, Rs. 500-800 for three-phase
  4. Income Tax: 7.5% for consumers using over 200 units
  5. FCA (Optional): Fuel Cost Adjustment varies monthly (±Rs. 2-5 per unit)

Accuracy Note

This calculator provides estimates based on current tariff rates. Actual bills may vary slightly due to QTA (Quarterly Tariff Adjustment), area-specific surcharges, and other monthly adjustments announced by K-Electric.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is this K-Electric bill calculator?

Our calculator uses the official K-Electric tariff rates for 2026 and provides 95-98% accurate estimates. Minor variations may occur due to monthly FCA, QTA adjustments, and area-specific charges.

What is the difference between basic and advanced calculator?

The basic calculator provides quick estimates using standard rates. The advanced calculator allows you to include FCA (Fuel Cost Adjustment), choose meter type, and get more detailed breakdowns.

How can I reduce my K-Electric bill?

Use the comparison tool to see how reducing consumption affects your bill. Staying under 200 units keeps you in the "protected" category with lower rates. Also consider using appliances during off-peak hours (11 PM - 7 AM).

Does this calculator include all charges?

Yes! The calculator includes energy charges, GST (17%), income tax (7.5% for 200+ units), meter rent, and optional FCA. It mirrors the actual KE billing system.
