What is Net Billing?

Net Billing is a billing mechanism under NEPRA's Prosumer Regulations, 2026 that credits solar energy system owners for the electricity they add to the grid. For example, if a residential customer has a PV system on their roof, it may generate more electricity than the home uses during daylight hours. If the home is net-metered, the electricity meter will run backwards to provide a credit against what electricity is consumed at night or other periods where the home's electricity use exceeds the system's output.

Application Process

Step 1: Select a Vendor

Choose a vendor certified by PPIB (Private Power and Infrastructure Board) for solar installation. Vendors are listed in three categories: C-1 (above 500 kW), C-2 (up to 500 kW), C-3 (up to 250 kW).

Step 2: Load Assessment

The vendor will assess your sanctioned load. You may need to upgrade your load if your solar system capacity is higher.

Step 3: Submit Application

Submit the application to K-Electric along with:

  • CNIC Copy
  • Latest Electricity Bill
  • Load Sanction Document
  • Technical Data of Solar Panels & Inverter

Step 4: Technical Review & NOC

KE will review the application and issue a NOC (No Objection Certificate).

Step 5: Installation & Metering

Once approved, the vendor installs the system. KE then replaces your standard meter with a Green Net Meter (bi-directional meter).

FAQs

Can I install net billing on a single-phase connection?

No, you must have a 3-phase connection to apply for net billing. You can apply for a phase conversion if needed.

How much roof space do I need?

Generally, 1kW of solar panels requires about 100 sq. ft. of shadow-free roof space.
