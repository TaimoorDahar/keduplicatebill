K-Electric Refund of Rs5.02 Per Unit in June 2026

Karachi, May 15, 2026 — K-Electric (KE) has submitted a petition to NEPRA asking for approval to refund Rs5.02 per unit to consumers through their June 2026 electricity bills. The company says lower fuel costs in March 2026 are behind the proposed relief.

According to KE's petition, the Fuel Charges Adjustment (FCA) for March reflects lower generation costs compared to the reference tariff previously charged to consumers. If approved, this refund would result in a cumulative financial relief of Rs6.79 billion for consumers in Karachi.

NEPRA scheduled a public hearing on May 22, 2026, to review the refund proposal. The benefit will not apply to lifeline users, protected domestic consumers, prepaid meter holders, or electric vehicle (EV) charging stations.

This marks the seventh consecutive monthly relief granted under the FCA mechanism, continuing a downward trend that began in September 2025. Over the past several months, KE has refunded amounts ranging from Rs0.17/unit to over Rs3/unit, with the proposed Rs5.02/unit for March being the largest yet.

Shift Towards National Grid Energy

The refund is largely attributed to KE's increased reliance on electricity sourced from the national grid, which has proven more cost-effective than local generation. During March, KE procured 55% of its total energy from the national grid, significantly reducing its dependence on expensive furnace oil and diesel-based generation.

This strategic shift has enabled KE to pass on savings to consumers while maintaining a stable power supply across Karachi. The company continues to work on optimizing its energy mix to ensure affordability and reliability for its customer base.

Key Points

  • Proposed refund: Rs5.02 per unit
  • Total relief: Rs6.79 billion
  • Public hearing: May 22, 2026
  • Seventh consecutive monthly relief
  • 55% energy from national grid in March

Frequently Asked Questions

Who qualifies for this refund?
Most regular KE consumers qualify. The refund does not apply to lifeline users, protected domestic consumers, prepaid meter holders, or EV charging stations.
How will the refund appear on my bill?
If NEPRA approves the petition, the credit will appear automatically in your June 2026 bill. You do not need to apply for it separately.
What is the Fuel Charges Adjustment (FCA)?
The FCA is a monthly adjustment based on actual fuel costs. When KE's generation costs fall below the reference tariff, consumers get a refund. When costs rise, a surcharge is added.
Why are KE's fuel costs lower right now?
KE sourced 55% of its energy from the national grid in March 2026, which is cheaper than running local furnace oil or diesel generators. Those savings are being passed on to consumers.
