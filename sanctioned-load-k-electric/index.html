K-Electric Sanctioned Load: What It Is and How to Manage It

Your K-Electric sanctioned load is the maximum power limit — measured in kilowatts (kW) — that KE has approved for your connection. It determines your meter type, your billing category, and whether you qualify as a protected or unprotected consumer.

What is Sanctioned Load with K-Electric?

K-Electric customers are allowed a maximum amount of electricity (measured in kilowatts, kW) at any given time. KE sets a predetermined limit based on your application and approves it.

How Does Sanctioned Load Work?

  • Application: When you apply for or request a new electricity connection, you specify how much power you anticipate needing (e.g., for domestic appliances or commercial equipment).
  • Approval: KE assesses your application and estimates electricity consumption based on appliance usage. Accordingly, they sanction a load limit.
  • Importance: The sanctioned load plays a role in several aspects of your K-Electric service.

Key Aspects of Sanctioned Load:

  • Billing: K-Electric has different billing structures based on sanctioned load. For example, there might be fixed charges per kW for exceeding the sanctioned load limit.
  • Metering: Your meter capacity is typically chosen based on your sanctioned load to ensure it can handle the maximum allowed power.
  • Power Supply: The type of power supply you receive (single-phase or three-phase) might depend on your sanctioned load (e.g., three-phase for loads exceeding a certain limit).

Sanctioned Load vs. Actual Consumption

It's important to understand that your sanctioned load is the maximum limit, but your actual electricity consumption might be lower. You only pay for the units (kWh) you use.

You can contact K-Electric if your sanctioned load exceeds your actual needs. You may be able to reduce your fixed charges.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is sanctioned load in K-Electric?
Sanctioned load is the maximum amount of electricity (in kW) that K-Electric has approved for your connection. It is set when you apply for a new connection, based on the appliances you plan to use. It affects your meter type, billing category, and supply type.
How do I check my sanctioned load?
Your sanctioned load is printed on your K-Electric bill in the consumer details section. You can also check it by logging into ke.com.pk, calling the 118 helpline, or visiting a KE customer service centre with your account number.
What if I use more than the sanctioned load?
Exceeding your sanctioned load can result in additional fixed charges per kW over the limit. In repeated or severe cases, K-Electric may disconnect your supply to protect the grid. If your usage has permanently increased, it is better to apply for a load increase.
What is protected and unprotected in K-Electric?
Protected consumers are residential customers with a sanctioned load below 5 kW who consistently use less than 200 units per month — they get lower, subsidised rates. Unprotected consumers are those who have crossed the 200-unit limit in the past six months and are billed at higher slab rates.
How do I apply to increase my sanctioned load?
You can apply online at ke.com.pk under My Services, or visit a KE customer service centre. You will need your CNIC, account number, and a list of the additional appliances that require the higher load.
Can I reduce my sanctioned load to save money?
Yes. If your actual usage is regularly lower than your sanctioned load, you can apply to reduce it. A lower sanctioned load can reduce the fixed charges on your monthly bill. Call 118 or visit a KE customer service centre to submit a reduction request.
Does sanctioned load affect my meter type?
Yes. Connections up to around 5–7 kW typically use a single-phase meter. If your sanctioned load is above that, K-Electric usually installs a three-phase meter to safely handle the higher demand.
